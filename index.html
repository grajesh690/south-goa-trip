Day-by-Day Itinerary

Arrive
Arrive, Decompress & First Taste
▾
"Land, breathe, feel the ocean air. South Goa doesn't rush you in — and you shouldn't either."
2:00 PM
🏖️
Hollant Beach
Dabolim · en route south
A perfect pit-stop on the way down from the airport. Utterly quiet, shallow water, zero vendors. Kids can splash safely while you shake off the flight.
💡 Also stop by SAHASEA Watersports here — book a sunrise kayaking slot for Day 2 or 3 in advance. Family-run, wonderful with kids.
5:00 PM
🌊
Check In · Walk Your Base Beach
Agonda or Palolem
Agonda is quieter and more family-appropriate. Palolem has more restaurants within walking distance. Both are excellent. Walk the beach at golden hour — it sets the tone.
7:30 PM
🌱
Dinner at Zest Vegan & Vegetarian
Agonda Beach Road
One of the best pure veg restaurants in South Goa. Ramen, summer rolls, green pakoras with kale & kimchi, and a legendary 3-layered chocolate mousse. Excellent smoothies. Open 8 AM–10 PM daily.
2
Beaches + Culture
Hidden Beaches & Feni Tasting
▾
"Two beaches nobody talks about, and the one feni experience every visitor to Goa should have."
8:30 AM
🏊
Cola Beach
Kola, Canacona
South Goa's crown jewel. A rare freshwater lagoon meets the Arabian Sea — completely unique in India. Kayaking in the lagoon (₹800/hr). Short 1.5 km trail from parking. Swimming restricted on the sea side but the lagoon is calm and magical.
💡 Go before 9 AM for zero crowd. The trail is easy; wear sandals. Shared jeeps available from parking for ₹100/person if you don't want to walk.
11:30 AM
🪨
Pebbles Beach via Cabo de Rama Fort
Cabo de Rama, Canaguinim
Not sand — a dramatic pebble shoreline below crumbling Portuguese fort walls. The walk down is steep and rugged (keeps crowds away). Combine with exploring the Cabo de Rama Fort ruins above — excellent for kids to clamber around.
4:00 PM
🥃
Cazulo Premium Feni — Floating Feni Experience
Cuelim, near Dabolim
Book this in advance (online). Host Tyson's storytelling about cashew feni history is reportedly one-of-a-kind — reviews consistently call it the highlight of their Goa trip. Adults get a tasting + cocktail pairing; the farm setting is green and relaxed for kids. Rated 4.8 ⭐ by 136 reviews.
💡 This is THE place for feni — not a shack shot. The experience includes food pairing and lasts ~2 hrs. Book at least 3 days ahead.
3
Villages + Backwaters
Sadolxem, Patnem & a Slow Evening
▾
"A morning at an old colonial bridge where the river bends through jungle — then a slow beach lunch and a dinner that feels like home."
9:00 AM
🌉
Sadolxem Bridge
Delem Village, Canacona
A gorgeous old bridge over a tidal creek, framed by dense jungle — one of the most photogenic spots in the south that almost no tourist visits. Boating and kayaking available on the river (₹1,500 for boat; negotiate). Best in soft morning light. Rated 4.5 ⭐.
💡 The bridge is narrow — park before it and walk across. Go on weekdays for full solitude.
12:30 PM
🥗
Lunch at Bibhitaki Vegan & Vegetarian Café
Patnem Main Road, Palolem
A beloved vegan café with indoor AC and a lovely courtyard. Rainbow Quinoa Bowl, Hawaiian Burger, and their almond milk hot chocolate are crowd favourites. Very family-friendly, calm, and well-priced. Rated 4.6 ⭐ by 1,800+ reviews.
2:30 PM
🌊
Patnem Beach (afternoon swim)
Patnem, Canacona
Quieter and more intimate than Palolem. Small, calm, safe for kids to splash around. Walk distance from Bibhitaki — just wander down after lunch.
7:00 PM
🍽️
Dinner at Cafe Barefoot – Wholesomely Vegetarian
Near Palolem, Devabag
A hidden pocket with 4.9 stars. Purely vegetarian, fresh, comforting food. The Adai Dosa here comes with a story about its Tamil heritage — perfect for you! Also try the Mumbai street sandwich and Belgian waffle. Open 11 AM–11 PM.
💡 The mountain backdrop and open space make it a calm dinner spot for families. No music blasting.
4
Hinterland + Water Sports
Spice Farm Deep Dive & Sundowner
▾
"Head inland through jungle hills to one of Goa's last authentic spice farms — then return to the sea for the most family-friendly water sports in the south."
10:00 AM
🌿
Tanshikar Spice Farm
Netravli, near Palolem
The best spice farm in South Goa — small, family-run, zero tourist bus crowds. The jungle drive up is itself scenic. Guided tour of cinnamon, cardamom, pepper, vanilla, cacao, and beekeeping. Ends with a delicious veg thali lunch served fresh. Rated 4.7 ⭐ by 791 reviews. Book ahead.
💡 Total cost ~₹750/person including lunch & tour. Allow 3 hrs. The nearby Netravli Wildlife Sanctuary is accessible if you want a light waterfall trek after.
3:00 PM
🏄
Prince of Sal Water Sports – Mobor Beach
Mobor, Cavelossim
Highly rated (4.8 ⭐, 664 reviews), no pushy tactics, professionally trained staff. Mobor itself is a calm, uncrowded beach. Activities: parasailing, jet ski, banana ride, ringo ride — all kid-appropriate. Go 3–5 PM for cooler temperatures.
6:30 PM
🍹
Sunset at The Southern Deck – Benaulim
Benaulim Beach, Goa
A proper beachfront restobar with live music evenings, great cocktails, and veg options. Book a table in advance for the sunset-facing outdoor section. Pomfret recheado, butter garlic prawns, Poloma Fresca cocktail. Rated 4.4 ⭐ by 7,700+ reviews.
💡 Closed on Tuesdays. Book by calling ahead — outdoor sunset tables fill fast on weekends.
5
Last Morning
Kakolem (Tiger Beach) & Farewell Lunch
▾
"Save the most dramatic beach for last. A steep jungle staircase — and a waterfall on the sand. Then one final pure veg meal before the drive north."
8:00 AM
🌊
Kakolem Beach (Tiger Beach)
Canacona, South Goa
South Goa's most dramatic hidden beach. A 15-minute steep staircase descent through jungle — and at the bottom, one of the cleanest, emptiest beaches in India with a waterfall literally on the sand. Jaw-dropping. Rated 4.7 ⭐. Almost zero crowd because of the hike.
💡 Wear closed shoes for the descent. No lifeguards — swim with caution, currents can be strong. Go early and have the whole place to yourself for an hour.
12:30 PM
🌿
Farewell Lunch – The Veggie Vibes, Varca
Varca, near airport belt
Pure veg, Jain, and vegan. Excellent chaap biryani, paneer tikka, veg Kolhapuri and dal tadka. Warm family-run service. Conveniently located near the Varca/Benaulim/airport corridor for a smooth departure.

🏄 Beach Activities

🚣
Sea Kayaking
SAHASEA · Hollant Beach
Family-run. Sunrise kayaking excellent with kids (a 6-yr-old reviewed it!). Instructor Callum is patient and great. ₹800/hr approx.
🛶
Lagoon Kayaking
Cola Beach · Kola
Paddle on the freshwater lagoon where a river meets the sea. Incredibly calm, scenic, unique. ₹800/hr.
🪂
Parasailing
Prince of Sal · Mobor Beach
4.8 ⭐ rated. Professional staff, no pushy upselling. Mobor is calm and uncrowded — the best spot for water sports in South Goa.
🛥️
Jet Ski / Banana / Ringo Ride
Prince of Sal · Mobor Beach
All family-safe options available. Kids can do banana and ringo rides. Book directly at the beach — no online middleman markup.
⛵
River Boating
Sadolxem Bridge
Boat rides on a calm tidal creek through jungle. Negotiate price (₹1,500 for a boat). Peaceful, photogenic, no crowds.
🏊
Safe Swimming Beaches
Agonda · Patnem · Hollant
All three are calm, shallow, and family-safe. Avoid Cola sea-side and Kakolem for swimming (strong currents). Agonda has the longest stretch.

💡 Practical Tips

📅
Best Season
October–March is ideal. April is warm but quieter (good for deals). Avoid June–September (monsoon — most shacks close).
🚗
Getting Around
Hire a local driver for ₹3,000–4,000/day — best for families. Or self-drive (Zoomcar/Drivezy). Scooters don't work well with a kid.
🏡
Where to Stay
Agonda = quieter, fewer crowds, best for families. Palolem = more restaurants walkable. Both are great. Stay 5 nights at one base — no need to move.
📞
Book in Advance
Cazulo Feni experience (book online), Tanshikar Spice Farm (call ahead), The Southern Deck (sunset table). Everything else can be spontaneous.
🌴
Finding Toddy
No tourist signs — just ask your driver for a local toddy shop near Cuncolim or Loutolim. Fresh neera costs ₹20–30. Drink at roadside under a tin roof.
☀️
Beach Timing
Go to hidden beaches (Cola, Kakolem) before 9 AM for zero crowd. Afternoons are for swimming on calmer beaches (Agonda, Patnem, Hollant).
💰
Budget Rough Guide
Spice Farm: ₹750/head · Feni Experience: ~₹2,000/couple · Water Sports: ₹2,000–3,000 for all 3 · Veg meals: ₹400–800/meal for family.
🧒
Kid-Friendly Picks
Tanshikar Farm, SAHASEA kayaking, Cabo de Rama Fort ruins, Cola lagoon, Hollant Beach (shallow water), Banana/Ringo rides at Mobor.
