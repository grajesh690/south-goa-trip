🌊 Bioluminescence Night Kayaking (If Dark Moon)
Talpona / Galgibaga backwaters or Hollant Beach
The Talpona and Galgibaga rivers near Palolem light up with bioluminescent plankton — every paddle stroke and splash glows electric blue. This is real, not a light show.

🌙 Bioluminescence — What You Need to Know

Best in Oct–Nov and sometimes Jan–Feb on new/dark moon nights. Not guaranteed every night — depends on plankton bloom and zero light pollution. Book via SAHASEA (Hollant) or Prashant Kayaking & Bike Rental (Talpona Beach) — WhatsApp ahead asking specifically for "bioluminescence kayaking" and confirm the moon phase. Budget ₹1,000–1,500/person. If the moon is too bright, reschedule to the darkest night of your trip.

📞 SAHASEA: +91 86693 94907  |  Prashant (Talpona): +91 79727 83898

✈️ Taking Liquor from Goa to Tamil Nadu — The Rules

✅ Legal Allowance: Tamil Nadu allows 2 litres of IMFL (Indian Made Foreign Liquor) or feni per adult for personal use without a permit or declaration. With 2 adults, you can bring 4 litres total comfortably within limits.
✈️ How to carry on a flight: Always pack in checked-in baggage only — never cabin/hand luggage (DGCA rules). Wrap bottles well in clothes or bubble wrap (bottle bags available at Goa airport shops). Bottles must be sealed. Keep within your total baggage weight limit.
📦 Packaging tip: Ask at Cazulo and Vaz Enterprises for travel-sealed gift packaging specifically for the flight. Tamper-evident seals help with both safety and customs optics.
🧾 Keep your receipt from the shop — especially for premium or multiple bottles. You won't need to declare for personal quantities, but it's good to have proof of purchase.
🎁 Smart buys for TN: Cashew Feni (GI product, unique to Goa, can't get elsewhere) · Coconut Feni · Paul John Indian Single Malt (40–50% cheaper than TN price) · Premium rums or gins at Goa rates.
⚠️ Beyond 2L/adult is a grey area. Air arrivals in Chennai are rarely checked for personal quantities, but legally, anything beyond 2L per adult is liable to confiscation under TN Prohibition Act. Don't risk it — stick to 2L per adult and enjoy the savings on what you do bring.

💡 Practical Tips

📅
Best Season
Oct–Mar ideal. Nov is best for bioluminescence. April is warm but quieter and cheaper. Avoid June–Sept (monsoon — beaches close, shacks shut).
🏡
Where to Stay
Agonda = quietest, lifeguards, family-perfect. Palolem = more walkable restaurants. Stay in one base the full 5 nights — no need to move. Both are 20 min apart by scooter.
🚗
Getting Around
Self-drive car (Nanu/Diljit) for full itinerary flexibility. Or Lavesh (Love Star Cab) as a dedicated driver. Teju/Rohan for a scooter for local beach hops. Don't rely on local autos — they're scarce in South Goa.
📞
Book in Advance

Bioluminescence Timing
Check moon phase before you travel. Schedule your night kayak on the darkest night of your trip (new moon ±3 days). Oct–Nov is peak season for it.
☀️
Beach Timing
Hidden beaches (Cola, Kakolem) before 9 AM — zero crowd. Dolphin boat trips leave 6:30–7 AM. Afternoons for swimming at Agonda/Patnem/Hollant (calm waves).
🧒
Best Spots for the Kid
Tanshikar Farm · Cotigao butterfly park · Cabo de Rama fort ruins · Cola lagoon kayak · SAHASEA sunrise kayak · Banana/Ringo rides at Mobor · Big Foot Museum audio guide.
